Frequently Asked Questions

My flowers are falling off or dying?

Flowers drop during high & cooler temperatures following blossom set can also lead to healthy blossoms falling off. Insufficient light, whether it's too much or too little, can also contribute to healthy flowers dropping off plants.

How do I choose a plant?

Check for insects by looking at the underside of leaves.Avoid plants with drought stress, indicated by yellow foliage.Be sure your container has a drainage hole in it, since plants don't like to sit in water.Add gravel to the bottom of the pot if it has no holes, and set the plant on top of the rocks.

What causes leaves to become pale?

Pale or yellow leaves can indicate several things. Are those leaves at the base of the plant, i.e. older leaves. If they fall off, the plant might not be receiving enough water. If it is sufficiently watered, it may be missing nitrogen. Add compost or fertilizer to the soil around the plant.

How often should you change plant pots?

On average, mature plants should be repotted less frequently than young, faster-growing plants. Young plants may need to be repotted every 12 to 18 months, yet older plants that grow more slowly may be repotted every 2 to 3 years, or as needed.

What causes brown crispy leaves?

Underwatering is one cause of why plants may be exhibiting dry and crispy leaves. Often times, if you let your houseplant's soil get bone dry, especially for extended periods, the lower leaves will typically turn brown and crispy. Either just the edges, or even the whole leaf.

Why are gnats flying around my plant?

The most common cause of fungus gnats in plants is overwatering, so be sure to let your soil dry out in between waterings as often as possible. Check the soil with your finger every few days and once you feel it starting to get dry, leave it like that for about a week to keep gnats away.
