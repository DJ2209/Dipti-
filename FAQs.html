Frequently Asked Questions

My flowers are falling off or dying?

Flowers drop during high & cooler temperatures following blossom set can also lead to healthy blossoms falling off. Insufficient light, whether it's too much or too little, can also contribute to healthy flowers dropping off plants.

How do I choose a plant?

Check for insects by looking at the underside of leaves.Avoid plants with drought stress.Be sure your container has a drainage hole in it.

What causes leaves to become pale?

Are those leaves at the base of the plant, i.e. older leaves. If they fall off, the plant might not be receiving enough water. If it is sufficiently watered, it may be missing nitrogen. Add compost or fertilizer to the soil around the plant.

How often should you change plant pots?

On average, mature plants should be repotted less frequently than young, faster-growing plants. Young plants may need to be repotted every 12 to 18 months, yet older plants that grow more slowly may be repotted every 2 to 3 years, or as needed.

What causes brown crispy leaves?

Underwatering is one cause of why plants may be exhibiting dry and crispy leaves. Often times, if you let your houseplant's soil get bone dry,the lower leaves will typically turn brown and crispy.

Why are gnats flying around my plant?

The most common cause of fungus gnats in plants is overwatering, so be sure to let your soil dry out in between waterings as often as possible.
